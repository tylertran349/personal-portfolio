About Me

I have been fascinated by technology since seventh grade. I used to spend my free time doing things like watching PC component review videos, overclocking my CPU and graphics card, and building computers on occasion. The most ambitious thing I have done is flash a modded VBIOS (BIOS of a graphics card) onto the GTX 970M on my ASUS gaming laptop to bypass the overclocking and voltage limits set by ASUS.

In middle school, I took classes at my local library that covered the basics of languages like Python, Java, and HTML. The first time I went in-depth into a programming language was my senior year of high school when I took AP Computer Science A, in which I learned intermediate Java concepts and essential computer science topics. Then, in August 2022, I started doing The Odin Project, a self-guided full-stack web development curriculum, in which I learned various full-stack web development languages, tools, and concepts and created 15+ projects. I love full-stack web development because it allows me to use my artistic ability, creativity, and problem-solving ability to make real-world websites and apps. Currently, I am a rising senior majoring in Computer Science at UC Davis and on track to graduate in June 2025.

When I'm not coding, I like to explore the world virtually in Flight Simulator 2020 and keep up with all things NFL-related. My favorite time of the year is NFL draft season where I get to think about what I would do if I was the GM of the San Francisco 49ers.
